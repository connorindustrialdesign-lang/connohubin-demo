Nightstand

a bedside object exploring lighting, storage, and tactile interaction

Problem

Night routines often involve switching between lighting, charging devices, and keeping small objects organized in low-light environments. Nightstand investigates how one form can simplify that interaction while maintaining a calm visual presence in a bedroom space.

IMAGE 1
PROCESS

Functions

the essential bedside functions integrated into a single object

  • Ambient lighting for night use
  • Inductive charger for wireless phone charging
  • Stable top surface for daily items
  • Compact footprint with clear access
  • Readable form language in low light

Organization features include:

  • Phone
  • Wallet
  • Keys
  • Cup holder
  • Pen

Material and Lighting

concrete, walnut, 1" steel tubing, and arcylic

The lighting is built into the surface of the nightstand to provide ambient lighting to the room as well as highlight the items on your nightstand. The contrast between lights-on and lights-off states helps communicate control, clarity, and mood.
