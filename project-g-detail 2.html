Project G

An innovative UV sanitation system designed to address global medical supply sterilization challenges.

The Problem

Extracted research data reveals the global scale of the challenge: 33% of medical clinics worldwide lack access to running water.

Without reliable water access, sterilizing medical tools becomes difficult, facilitating disease spread and bacterial contamination in clinical environments.

Pura addresses this challenge through an alternative sterilization approach suitable for resource-constrained regions.

Research & Insight

UV light kills 100% of bacteria—a vastly superior outcome compared to alternative sterilization methods. More critically, UV sterilization takes only 60 seconds and costs just $0.15 per cycle.

This combination of efficacy, speed, and affordability makes UV a viable solution for clinics operating under extreme resource constraints.

Design Iterations

Development included a proof-of-concept "shoebox" form factor for portability and ease of distribution. Strategic partnerships—especially with Tesla—leveraged expertise in electrical systems and power management.

Control interfaces and lighting states were explored to ensure clinicians could reliably operate the system and verify sterilization cycles.

Final Product

The sterilization system in its final product configurations shows the hinged lid open and closed states, demonstrating the integrated control interface and effective form language for clinical use.
